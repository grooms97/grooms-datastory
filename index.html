My Data Story: Twitter

The 5-day journey by L. Grooms

  • The Data Collection

    I tracked my Twitter usage for 5 days: Monday-Friday. I payed attention to factors including the timestamp, my intention, my mood, the device I was using at the time (i.e. IPhone or laptop), my location, the amount of time I spent using it, the account handle (i.e. @NBCNews), the account status (i.e. verified), whether or not I was following the account, the media (i.e. image, video), the topic and subject, and tweet interaction (i.e. 350 retweets).

    In Figure 1, you will see the data visual that was created from this data. It was created using InkScape. I believe that I found out a good amount about my usage habits. For example, it looks as though, the majority of the time, I am either tired or content. During these moods, I am viewing (almost) all verified accounts. This could be due to the fact that I am seeking out waking up and seeing what I have missed in the news--this would involve verified accounts. I also tend to turn to Twitter when I am content, looking for accounts of celebrities to entertain me.

    I also found out quite a bit about the device I use to access Twitter. Evidently, I use my phone to access Twitter more than I use my laptop. I believe this is because my phone is always on my person--my laptop is not. It's much easier to pull up the Twitter application on my IPhone when I am waiting for class than it is to whip out my laptop.

  • The Takeaway

    Overall, this project has convinced me that I should change my habits. After viewing the timestamps, I realized that I spend a lot of needless time on Twitter throughout the day. I need to cut back and spend more time with the people around me.

    Additionally, I should start viewing more accounts that aren't verified. This would include many of my friends' accounts. It would distract me from negative news and I believe that it could uplift my mood.
